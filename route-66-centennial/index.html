Why Run?

If my previous ultra-distance projects have taught me anything, it's that running is the most intimate way to travel a landscape. I am drawn to the intensity and vulnerability that go hand-in-hand with self-supported long-distance running; when the effort of movement strips you raw, every pebble underfoot lodges in your brain, and every human interaction becomes a core memory. It's not a runner's high - rather, it's the purity of experience that comes from becase such circumstances create vivid experiences a navigating self-supported by foot across the vast open landscapes of America creates an intensity and a vulnerability of experience that fosters memorable and meaningful experiences
